## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI represents a significant paradigm shift from traditional AI models confined to digital environments. It encompasses AI systems with physical form that interact with and operate in the real world. This embodied approach offers several advantages:

- **Real-world Grounding**: Physical AI systems understand the world through direct interaction rather than abstract data
- **Embodied Cognition**: Intelligence emerges from the interaction between the agent's physical structure and its environment
- **Human-Centered Design**: Humanoid robots can operate in human-centered environments more effectively

### The Embodiment Hypothesis

The embodiment hypothesis suggests that intelligence emerges not just from computational power, but from the interaction between brain, body, and environment. This perspective recognizes that the body itself can serve as a computational resource, with the physical structure constraining and enabling certain behaviors and cognitive processes.

## Why Humanoid Robotics?

Humanoid robots are poised to excel in our human-centered world because they share our physical form and can be trained with abundant data from interacting in human environments. This represents a significant transition from AI models confined to digital environments to embodied intelligence that operates in physical space.

Key advantages of humanoid robots:
- **Environmental Compatibility**: Designed to operate in spaces built for humans
- **Social Interaction**: Human-like form facilitates natural human-robot interaction
- **Learning Efficiency**: Can leverage human behavioral patterns and training data
